# Requirements Document

## Introduction

This document specifies the end-to-end pipeline for provAI's core feature: from photo capture through body measurement extraction, deterministic size recommendation, virtual try-on image generation, and styling advice delivery. provAI is a B2B SaaS platform for fashion consultants and stores. Its key differentiator over competitors (Zara virtual try-on, AI Wardrobe, Futurize.io) is combining a diffusion-model virtual try-on with a deterministic, math-based size recommendation that shows the user exactly why a given size was chosen (e.g., "Tamanho M porque busto 92cm está na faixa M (88-94cm)").

The pipeline supports three input modes: photo with physical marker calibration (highest accuracy), photo with user-provided height (good accuracy), and manual measurement entry (no photo needed). All measurement calculations are deterministic TypeScript math — no AI guessing. The virtual try-on image is generated by FASHN AI (diffusion model), and styling advice is provided by Google Gemini as a complementary, non-critical step.

## Glossary

- **Pipeline**: The complete sequence of steps from photo capture to final result display (try-on image + size + styling tips).
- **Capture_Guide**: The camera overlay system including the body silhouette, postural grid, and 8-point validation checklist that guides the user to take a properly framed photo.
- **Scale_Calibration**: The process of establishing a pixels-to-centimeters ratio (px_per_cm) using either a physical marker (credit card, A4 paper, banknote) or the user's declared height.
- **Landmark_Detector**: The Gemini 2.0 Flash API call that receives a photo and returns body landmark coordinates in pixels (x, y). It does NOT calculate measurements or sizes.
- **Measurement_Engine**: The local TypeScript module (bodyMeasurement.ts) that converts pixel-space landmarks into centimeter measurements using the px_per_cm calibration factor, anthropometric circumference factors (ISO 8559), and the Ramanujan ellipse formula for lateral photos.
- **Size_Engine**: The local TypeScript module (sizingTables.ts + sizing.ts) that compares centimeter measurements against ABNT NBR 13377 sizing tables to produce a deterministic size recommendation (P/M/G/GG or numeric).
- **ABNT_Tables**: Brazilian sizing standard tables (ABNT NBR 13377) for female and male tops and bottoms, including brand-specific adjustment offsets.
- **Tryon_Generator**: The FASHN AI v1.6 API integration (Supabase Edge Function) that generates a virtual try-on image showing the user wearing a selected garment using a diffusion model.
- **Styling_Advisor**: The Gemini-based complementary analysis that provides body type classification, fit notes, color harmony assessment, and outfit combination suggestions.
- **Garment_Image**: The image of the clothing item to be virtually tried on, obtained either by direct upload or by scraping a product URL.
- **Brand_Adjustment**: A sizing offset applied when the garment is from a known brand with non-standard sizing (e.g., Zara runs small → size up one).
- **Consultant**: A fashion consultant or store employee who uses provAI as a B2B tool to serve their clients.
- **Client**: The end person whose body is being measured and who will see the try-on result. May be the Consultant themselves or a customer.
- **Hem_Preference**: The user's preferred trouser/dress length (ankle, 7/8, cropped, floor, midi, knee) used to calculate hem adjustment recommendations.

## Requirements

### Requirement 1: Guided Photo Capture

**User Story:** As a Consultant, I want the camera interface to guide the Client into the correct pose and framing, so that the landmark detection produces accurate pixel coordinates.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. WHEN the Client opens the camera capture screen, THE Capture_Guide SHALL display a semi-transparent body silhouette overlay matching the selected variant (front or side).
2. WHILE the camera is active, THE Capture_Guide SHALL run an 8-point validation checklist evaluating head visibility, shoulder alignment, full body in frame, arms slightly away from torso, adequate lighting, stable pose, no obstructing clothing, and feet visible.
3. WHEN all 8 checklist points pass, THE Capture_Guide SHALL enable the capture button and display a "ready" indicator.
4. WHEN fewer than 8 checklist points pass, THE Capture_Guide SHALL keep the capture button disabled and display which specific points are failing.
5. THE Capture_Guide SHALL support two capture variants: "front" for frontal photo and "side" for lateral profile photo.
6. WHEN the Client captures a photo, THE Capture_Guide SHALL compress the image to a maximum width of 1024 pixels and JPEG quality of 0.75 before sending it to the Landmark_Detector.

### Requirement 2: Scale Calibration

**User Story:** As a Consultant, I want to calibrate the pixel-to-centimeter scale using a physical marker or the Client's height, so that body measurements are converted accurately from pixels to real-world centimeters.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. WHEN the Client places a physical marker (credit card 85.6×53.98mm, A4 sheet 210×297mm, or Brazilian banknote 140×65mm) in the photo, THE Scale_Calibration SHALL detect the marker and compute px_per_cm with confidence "alta".
2. WHEN no physical marker is available, THE Scale_Calibration SHALL accept the Client's declared height in centimeters and compute px_per_cm by dividing the head-to-ankle pixel distance by the declared height, with confidence "media".
3. THE Scale_Calibration SHALL return a ScaleCalibration object containing px_per_cm (number), confidence ("alta" or "media"), and source ("marker" or "height").
4. IF the marker detection fails (marker not found or partially occluded), THEN THE Scale_Calibration SHALL fall back to height-based calibration and notify the user that marker detection failed.
5. IF neither a physical marker nor a declared height is provided, THEN THE Scale_Calibration SHALL prevent the pipeline from proceeding to measurement calculation and prompt the user to provide at least one calibration source.

### Requirement 3: Landmark Detection via Gemini

**User Story:** As a Consultant, I want the system to detect body landmarks from the Client's photo using Gemini AI, so that the Measurement_Engine has pixel coordinates to work with.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. WHEN a captured photo is submitted, THE Landmark_Detector SHALL send the image to the Gemini 2.0 Flash API with a prompt requesting ONLY pixel coordinates for 14 mandatory landmarks (head_top, chin, shoulder_left, shoulder_right, bust_left, bust_right, waist_left, waist_right, hip_left, hip_right, knee_left, knee_right, ankle_left, ankle_right) and 2 optional landmarks (wrist_left, wrist_right).
2. THE Landmark_Detector SHALL return a JSON object containing image_width_px, image_height_px, a landmarks object with [x, y] arrays, a confidence score (0–1), a pose_quality rating ("boa", "aceitavel", "ruim"), and an issues array listing detected problems.
3. THE Landmark_Detector SHALL NOT calculate measurements in centimeters, estimate sizes, or make any sizing recommendations — it returns pixel coordinates only.
4. IF the Landmark_Detector returns pose_quality "ruim", THEN THE Pipeline SHALL display the issues list to the user and recommend retaking the photo.
5. IF the Gemini API returns a safety block or empty response, THEN THE Landmark_Detector SHALL display a user-friendly error message and allow the user to retry with a different photo.
6. WHEN a lateral photo is also provided, THE Landmark_Detector SHALL additionally detect lateral landmarks (waist_front, waist_back, hip_front, hip_back, bust_front, bust_back) for use in the Ramanujan ellipse formula.

### Requirement 4: Deterministic Measurement Calculation

**User Story:** As a Consultant, I want body measurements to be calculated using deterministic math (not AI estimation), so that the results are reproducible, auditable, and trustworthy.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. WHEN landmarks and a ScaleCalibration are provided, THE Measurement_Engine SHALL calculate all linear measurements (height, shoulder width, inseam, torso length, arm length) by dividing pixel distances by px_per_cm.
2. WHEN only a frontal photo is available, THE Measurement_Engine SHALL calculate circumferences (bust, waist, hip, thigh, neck) by multiplying the frontal width in centimeters by the gender-specific anthropometric factor from ISO 8559 (e.g., female bust factor = 2.58, male bust factor = 2.72).
3. WHEN both frontal and lateral photos are available, THE Measurement_Engine SHALL calculate circumferences using the Ramanujan ellipse approximation: P ≈ π × (3(a+b) − √((3a+b)(a+3b))), where a = frontal_width/2 and b = lateral_depth/2.
4. THE Measurement_Engine SHALL calculate industrial tailoring measurements: crotch height (height × 16%), pants length (height × 61%), shirt length (height × 45%), and armhole depth (bust_cm / 4.4).
5. THE Measurement_Engine SHALL assign confidence "alta" when calibrated by physical marker and "media" when calibrated by declared height.
6. THE Measurement_Engine SHALL include explanatory notes in the output describing which calculation method was used (anthropometric factors vs. Ramanujan ellipse) and the calibration source.
7. FOR ALL valid BodyLandmarks and ScaleCalibration inputs, THE Measurement_Engine SHALL produce identical CalculatedMeasurements output when called multiple times with the same inputs (deterministic, no randomness).

### Requirement 5: Deterministic Size Recommendation

**User Story:** As a Consultant, I want the size recommendation to be based on transparent math comparing measurements against ABNT tables, so that the Client can see exactly why a specific size was chosen.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. WHEN measurements are provided, THE Size_Engine SHALL compare bust, waist, and hip circumferences against the ABNT NBR 13377 sizing tables for the specified gender.
2. THE Size_Engine SHALL apply the "largest measurement wins" rule: for tops, the size is determined by whichever of bust, waist, or hip falls in the largest size range; for bottoms, hip takes priority over waist.
3. THE Size_Engine SHALL return a SizeRecommendation containing size_brazil (PP/P/M/G/GG/XGG), size_international (XS/S/M/L/XL/XXL), size_european (34–48), pants_number_brazil (34–48), a justification string, and a confidence level.
4. THE Size_Engine SHALL format the justification as a human-readable explanation, for example: "Busto 92cm → M (88-94cm), Cintura 74cm → M (68-74cm), Quadril 100cm → M (94-100cm). Maior: M."
5. WHEN a known brand is identified from the product URL, THE Size_Engine SHALL apply the Brand_Adjustment offset (e.g., Zara: if between two sizes, size up one; Farm: tolerates more variation due to loose fit).
6. THE Size_Engine SHALL support both female sizing tables (tops: PP–XGG by bust/waist/hip; bottoms: 34–48 by waist/hip) and male sizing tables (tops: PP–XGG by chest/waist; bottoms: 36–48 by waist/hip).
7. FOR ALL valid measurement inputs, THE Size_Engine SHALL produce identical SizeRecommendation output when called multiple times with the same inputs (deterministic, no randomness).

### Requirement 6: Garment Image Resolution

**User Story:** As a Consultant, I want to provide a garment for virtual try-on either by uploading an image or pasting a product URL, so that the system can obtain the garment image flexibly.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. WHEN the Consultant uploads a garment image directly, THE Pipeline SHALL use that image as the garment input for the Tryon_Generator.
2. WHEN the Consultant provides a product URL instead of an image, THE Pipeline SHALL attempt to extract garment images from the URL using Firecrawl API (if configured) or HTML scraping as fallback.
3. WHEN extracting from a URL, THE Pipeline SHALL prioritize og:image meta tags, then img tags with jpg/jpeg/png/webp extensions, collecting up to 4 candidate images.
4. IF no garment image can be resolved from either upload or URL, THEN THE Pipeline SHALL return an error message asking the user to try another link or upload the garment image directly.
5. THE Pipeline SHALL validate that garment image URLs use http or https protocol and do not point to blocked hosts (localhost, private IP ranges, .local, .internal domains).
6. WHEN converting a garment URL to a data URL for the Tryon_Generator, THE Pipeline SHALL enforce a maximum image size of 6MB and a fetch timeout of 8 seconds.

### Requirement 7: Virtual Try-On Image Generation

**User Story:** As a Consultant, I want to see the Client wearing the selected garment in a realistic virtual try-on image, so that the Client can visualize how the garment looks on their body before purchasing.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. WHEN a user photo and a garment image are available, THE Tryon_Generator SHALL submit a try-on job to the FASHN AI v1.6 API with model_name "fashn/tryon", mode "balanced", and segmentation_free enabled.
2. THE Tryon_Generator SHALL poll the FASHN AI status endpoint every 2 seconds for up to 45 attempts (90 seconds maximum) until the job completes or fails.
3. WHEN the FASHN AI job completes successfully, THE Tryon_Generator SHALL return the generated try-on image (base64 JPEG).
4. THE Tryon_Generator SHALL support garment category selection: "auto" (default), "tops", "bottoms", or "one-pieces".
5. IF the FASHN AI API returns a 401 error, THEN THE Tryon_Generator SHALL return "FASHN API key inválida."
6. IF the FASHN AI API returns a 402 error, THEN THE Tryon_Generator SHALL return "Créditos FASHN esgotados."
7. IF the FASHN AI API returns a 429 error, THEN THE Tryon_Generator SHALL return "Limite FASHN atingido. Aguarde."
8. IF the FASHN AI job exceeds the 90-second polling timeout, THEN THE Tryon_Generator SHALL return a timeout error message.
9. THE Tryon_Generator SHALL consume 2 usage quota credits per try-on request.
10. WHEN the user has insufficient quota, THE Tryon_Generator SHALL return a quota exceeded response before calling the FASHN AI API.

### Requirement 8: Styling Advice Generation

**User Story:** As a Consultant, I want complementary styling advice (body type, fit notes, color harmony, outfit combinations), so that the Client receives a complete fashion consultation alongside the try-on image and size.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. WHEN the try-on image is generated successfully, THE Styling_Advisor SHALL call the Gemini API with the user photo, garment image, Client measurements, body type, and gender to generate styling advice.
2. THE Styling_Advisor SHALL return a JSON object containing: size_advice (string with justification), fit_notes (array of 3 fit observations), confidence ("alta", "media", "baixa"), combinations (array of outfit suggestions with occasion), and color_harmony (garment color analysis with skin tone compatibility).
3. IF the Styling_Advisor call fails, THEN THE Pipeline SHALL still display the try-on image and deterministic size recommendation — styling advice is non-blocking and complementary.
4. WHEN product context is available from URL scraping (brand, fabric, size chart), THE Styling_Advisor SHALL incorporate that context into its recommendations.

### Requirement 9: Manual Measurement Input Mode

**User Story:** As a Consultant, I want to enter the Client's measurements manually (without a photo), so that size recommendations work even when photo capture is not possible.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. WHEN the Consultant selects manual input mode, THE Pipeline SHALL present a form accepting: height (cm), weight (kg, optional), bust/chest circumference (cm), waist circumference (cm), hip circumference (cm), and inseam (cm, optional).
2. WHEN manual measurements are submitted, THE Size_Engine SHALL calculate the size recommendation using the same deterministic ABNT table comparison as the photo-based flow.
3. WHEN manual measurements are submitted, THE Pipeline SHALL skip the Landmark_Detector and Measurement_Engine steps and proceed directly to size recommendation.
4. WHEN manual measurements are submitted along with a garment image or URL, THE Pipeline SHALL still allow virtual try-on image generation using the user's photo (if provided separately) and the garment image.

### Requirement 10: Result Display

**User Story:** As a Consultant, I want the final result screen to show the try-on image, size recommendation with math justification, and styling tips in a clear layout, so that the Client has all the information needed to make a purchase decision.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. WHEN the pipeline completes, THE Result_Display SHALL show the virtual try-on image prominently.
2. THE Result_Display SHALL show the size recommendation including: Brazilian size (P/M/G/GG), international size (XS/S/M/L/XL), European size (34–48), and pants number (34–48).
3. THE Result_Display SHALL show the measurement-based justification string explaining why each size was chosen (e.g., "Busto 92cm → M (88-94cm), Cintura 74cm → M (68-74cm)").
4. THE Result_Display SHALL show the calculated body measurements (bust, waist, hip, shoulder width, inseam) with the confidence level and calculation method used.
5. WHEN styling advice is available, THE Result_Display SHALL show fit notes, outfit combinations, and color harmony analysis.
6. WHEN tailoring adjustments are relevant, THE Result_Display SHALL show hem adjustment recommendations (e.g., "Encurtar 3cm de barra") and armhole/cuff notes.
7. IF the try-on image generation failed but measurements and sizing succeeded, THEN THE Result_Display SHALL show the size recommendation and styling advice without the try-on image, along with an explanation of the try-on failure.

### Requirement 11: Privacy and Photo Handling

**User Story:** As a Consultant, I want to control whether the Client's photos are stored or discarded after analysis, so that the system complies with privacy regulations and Client consent preferences.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. THE Pipeline SHALL process photos in-memory during the analysis pipeline without persisting them to permanent storage by default.
2. WHEN the Consultant explicitly opts to save the analysis (with Client consent), THE Pipeline SHALL store the photo in Supabase Storage with the user's authentication context.
3. WHEN the analysis is complete and the Consultant has not opted to save, THE Pipeline SHALL discard the photo data from memory and not retain it in any server-side cache or log.
4. THE Pipeline SHALL NOT send the Client's photo to any third-party service other than Gemini (for landmarks) and FASHN AI (for try-on), both of which are required for the core pipeline functionality.
5. IF the Consultant attempts to save a photo without prior Client consent indication, THEN THE Pipeline SHALL display a consent confirmation dialog before proceeding with storage.

### Requirement 12: Authentication and Quota Enforcement

**User Story:** As a platform operator, I want the virtual try-on pipeline to require authentication and enforce usage quotas, so that the B2B billing model is respected and resources are not abused.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. WHEN a request arrives at the Tryon_Generator edge function without a valid Bearer token, THE Pipeline SHALL return a 401 Unauthorized response.
2. WHEN a request arrives with a valid Bearer token, THE Pipeline SHALL verify the user via Supabase Auth before processing.
3. WHEN the user's quota for "tryon" operations is insufficient (fewer than 2 credits remaining), THE Pipeline SHALL return a quota exceeded response with details about the current usage and plan limits.
4. WHEN the try-on request is processed successfully, THE Pipeline SHALL decrement the user's quota by 2 credits.
5. IF the FASHN AI call fails after quota has been decremented, THEN THE Pipeline SHALL NOT refund the quota credits automatically (failed attempts still consume credits due to external API costs).

### Requirement 13: Garment Category Detection

**User Story:** As a Consultant, I want the system to automatically detect whether a garment is a top, bottom, dress, or outerwear from the product name or description, so that the correct sizing table and fit logic are applied.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. WHEN a product name or description is provided, THE Size_Engine SHALL classify the garment into one of four categories: "top" (blusa, camisa, camiseta, regata, body, polo, cropped, suéter, moletom, cardigan), "bottom" (calça, jeans, short, bermuda, saia, legging, pantalona), "dress" (vestido, macacão, jumpsuit, macaquinho), or "outerwear" (blazer, casaco, jaqueta, trench, sobretudo, parka, colete).
2. WHEN the garment is classified as "top" or "outerwear", THE Size_Engine SHALL use bust and waist as the primary sizing measurements.
3. WHEN the garment is classified as "bottom", THE Size_Engine SHALL use hip as the priority measurement and waist as secondary.
4. WHEN the garment is classified as "dress", THE Size_Engine SHALL use bust as primary and flag when hip exceeds bust by more than 8cm (recommending sizing up for the skirt portion).
5. IF the garment cannot be classified from the text, THEN THE Size_Engine SHALL return category "unknown" and allow the Consultant to manually select the category.

### Requirement 14: Hem and Fit Preference Adjustments

**User Story:** As a Consultant, I want to specify the Client's preferred trouser length and fit preferences, so that the size recommendation includes tailoring adjustment notes.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. THE Size_Engine SHALL support six hem preferences: "floor" (2cm beyond ankle), "ankle" (reference length), "seven_eighths" (6cm above ankle), "cropped" (12cm above ankle), "midi" (knee to mid-calf, for dresses), and "knee" (at knee, for dresses).
2. WHEN a hem preference is selected, THE Size_Engine SHALL calculate the hem adjustment in centimeters based on the Client's inseam measurement and the selected preference offset.
3. WHEN the calculated hem adjustment exceeds 2cm, THE Size_Engine SHALL include a note such as "Barra (tornozelo): encurtar 3cm".
4. WHEN the garment category does not support the selected hem preference (e.g., "midi" for pants), THE Size_Engine SHALL resolve to the default preference for that category (ankle for bottoms, midi for dresses).

### Requirement 15: Measurement Engine Round-Trip Consistency

**User Story:** As a developer, I want to verify that the measurement and sizing pipeline produces consistent results, so that I can trust the deterministic guarantees of the system.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. FOR ALL valid BodyLandmarks inputs, THE Measurement_Engine SHALL produce the same CalculatedMeasurements when the same landmarks and calibration are provided (idempotent).
2. FOR ALL valid CalculatedMeasurements, THE Size_Engine SHALL produce the same SizeRecommendation when the same measurements and gender are provided (idempotent).
3. FOR ALL valid frontal width values and gender-specific factors, THE Measurement_Engine circumference calculation (width × factor) SHALL produce a result within the expected anthropometric range for that body region.
4. FOR ALL valid width and depth pairs, THE Ramanujan ellipse formula SHALL produce a circumference value greater than or equal to the simple rectangle perimeter (2 × (width + depth)) divided by π, and less than or equal to the rectangle perimeter (2 × (width + depth)).
